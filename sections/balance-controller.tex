\section{Balance Controller (Task 2)}
\label{sec:balance}

\paragraph{Plant.} With Task~1 closed, linearising \texttt{vel\_ref} $\to$ tilt gives a 7th-order $G_{tilt}(s)$ with one RHP pole at $\approx +8.9\,\mathrm{rad/s}$ (the inverted-pendulum falling mode). Plain PI-Lead cannot stabilise this -- Nyquist would not encircle $(-1,0)$ in the right direction. We follow Lecture~10 Method~2.

\paragraph{Step 1 -- Sign of $K_{PS}$.} $G_{tilt}$ has DC gain $> 0$ and $P = 1$. The criterion $Z = N + P$ with $Z = 0$ requires $N = -1$ (one CCW encirclement); positive $K_{PS}$ cannot produce this, so $\mathrm{sign}(K_{PS}) = -1$, absorbed into the post-integrator below.

\paragraph{Step 2 -- Post-integrator at the magnitude peak.} Place the PI zero at $\omega_\mathrm{peak}$ where $|G_{tilt}|$ peaks ($\omega_\mathrm{peak} = 8.03\,\mathrm{rad/s}$):
\begin{equation}
    \tau_{i,\mathrm{post}} = 1/\omega_\mathrm{peak} = 0.1245\,\mathrm{s}, \quad
    G_{tilt,\mathrm{post}}(s) = -\,C_{PI,\mathrm{post}}(s)\,G_{tilt}(s).
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Step 3 -- Outer PI-Lead.} Specs $\omega_c = 15\,\mathrm{rad/s}$, $\gamma_M = 60^{\circ}$, $N_i = 3$. PI zero at $\tau_i = 0.200\,\mathrm{s}$. Phase balance at $\omega_c$: $\angle G_{tilt,\mathrm{post}}(j15) = -135.1^{\circ}$, $\phi_{PI} = -18.4^{\circ}$, required $\phi_\mathrm{Lead} = +33.5^{\circ}$. The Lead is realised through the gyro ($\dot\theta = s\theta$ measured directly), giving the ideal form $(\tau_d s + 1)\theta = \tau_d \cdot \mathrm{gyro} + \theta$ with $\tau_d = \tan(33.5^{\circ})/15 = 0.0442\,\mathrm{s}$ -- no filter pole needed. From $|L(j\omega_c)| = 1$: $K_P = 1/0.833 = 1.1999$.
\begin{equation}
    C_\mathrm{tilt}(s) = -1.1999 \cdot \frac{0.1245 s + 1}{0.1245 s} \cdot \frac{0.2 s + 1}{0.2 s} \cdot (0.0442 s + 1).
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Step 4 -- Verify.} \texttt{margin(L\_tilt)}: $\omega_c = 15.00$, $\gamma_M = 60.00^{\circ}$, $GM = -5.58\,\mathrm{dB}$, 0 RHP closed-loop poles. Negative GM is the expected \emph{lower} bound on $|K|$ for unstable plants ($P = 1$). Linear IC ($\theta_0 = 10^{\circ}$) settles in $1.34\,\mathrm{s}$ with $\sim 6.6^{\circ}$ undershoot (RHP-zero signature). Simulink non-linear sim: $10^{\circ}$ recovery in $\sim 0.3\,\mathrm{s}$, peak motor voltage $2.8\,\mathrm{V}$.

\paragraph{Hardware (Test~3a).} Mission: \texttt{vel=0, bal=1, log=15 : time=10}. Robot held upright at the balance point, mission started, robot released. Balance held the full 10\,s, drift $0.343\,\mathrm{m}$ (spec $\leq 0.5$ \checkmark). Hardware matches the linear-IC settling prediction of $1.34\,\mathrm{s}$. \textbf{Firmware sign:} \texttt{[cbal] kp = -1.1999} -- the firmware does not absorb the Method~2 sign flip; positive $K_P$ produced a positive-feedback runaway in the first campaign.
