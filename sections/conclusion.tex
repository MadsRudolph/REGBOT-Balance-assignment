\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Four cascaded controllers were designed for the REGBOT using the frequency-domain phase-balance method and verified in Simulink on the non-linear Simscape Multibody model. The final gains committed to \texttt{regbot\_mg.m} are summarised in Table~\ref{tab:final_gains}.

        \hline
        1 -- Wheel speed & PI                 & $30\,\mathrm{rad/s}$  & $121.6^{\circ}$ & $K_P = 3.31$, $\tau_i = 0.10\,\mathrm{s}$ \\
        2 -- Balance     & PILead + Post-PI   & $15\,\mathrm{rad/s}$  & $60^{\circ}$    & $K_P = 1.137$, $\tau_i = 0.20\,\mathrm{s}$, $\tau_d = 0.1355\,\mathrm{s}$, $\tau_{i,\mathrm{post}} = 0.1682\,\mathrm{s}$ \\

        4 -- Position    & P                  & $0.6\,\mathrm{rad/s}$ & $59^{\circ}$    & $K_P = 0.534$ \\

The central challenge was Task~2, where the open-loop balance plant carries one RHP pole at $+8.7\,\mathrm{rad/s}$. Lecture~10 Method~2 solves this by (i) absorbing a sign flip into a post-integrator placed at the magnitude peak of $|G_{tilt}|$, producing a monotonically decreasing shaped plant $G_{tilt,\mathrm{post}}$, and (ii) designing an outer PI-Lead for the shaped plant using standard phase balance. The gyro signal was used to realise an ideal $(\tau_d s + 1)$ Lead without the filter pole of a proper Lead.

Task~3 is constrained by a real RHP zero at $+8.7\,\mathrm{rad/s}$ in the closed-balance plant --- the non-minimum-phase physics of the inverted pendulum. This limits $\omega_{c,\mathrm{vel}} \leq z/5 \approx 1.7\,\mathrm{rad/s}$; the conservative choice $\omega_{c,\mathrm{vel}} = 1\,\mathrm{rad/s}$ was taken for robustness. Task~4 exploits the free integrator already present in $G_{pos,\mathrm{outer}}$ and therefore requires only a proportional controller. To satisfy the mission peak-velocity specification ($v > 0.7\,\mathrm{m/s}$ on a 2\,m move), the outer-loop separation was pushed from the conservative $5\times$ down to $1.67\times$; the resulting design achieves $0.80\,\mathrm{m/s}$ peak velocity and $59^{\circ}$ phase margin.

All four Simulink simulations meet their respective specifications with comfortable headroom (no voltage saturation, pitch excursions below $10^{\circ}$, and zero steady-state error). The four designs were then ported to the physical REGBOT by loading them from a group configuration file (\texttt{config/regbot\_group47.ini}) into the firmware controller blocks through the REGBOT GUI, and validated with four lab tests (Tests~0, 3a, 3b, 4). The hardware results are summarised in Table~\ref{tab:hw_summary}.

Two practical findings emerged from the hardware work:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Sign of $K_P$ on the Balance block.} The firmware Balance controller does not internally absorb the Lecture~10 Method~2 minus sign; it has to be entered explicitly (\texttt{kp = -1.137} in \texttt{[cbal]}). An initial hardware run with positive $K_P$ produced the expected positive-feedback runaway. This is consistent with the theory --- the minus sign must live somewhere in the physical loop --- and the firmware leaves that choice to the designer.
    \item \textbf{Tilt-offset sensitivity.} A residual $0.78^{\circ}$ tilt-offset calibration error produces a $0.34\,\mathrm{m}$ drift over 10\,s in the balance-only test, and manifests as a small late-time limit cycle in the position test once the robot is near its target and wheel static friction dominates. A more careful tilt-offset calibration would suppress both effects; neither violates a listed specification.
\end{enumerate}

The overall outcome is that the four-layer cascade --- inner wheel-speed PI, balance PILead with sign-absorbing post-integrator, outer velocity PI, and outermost position P --- works both in simulation and on the physical robot. The XY-plane figure required by the assignment (Figure~\ref{fig:task3_hw_xy}) is a clean closed square with $359.8^{\circ}$ of cumulative heading change.
