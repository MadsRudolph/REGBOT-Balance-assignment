\section{Control Architecture}
\label{sec:architecture}

The cascade runs from outside in: position $\to$ velocity $\to$ tilt $\to$ wheel-velocity $\to$ motor voltage. Each outer loop's plant is the closed-loop response of the inner system, identified by linearising \texttt{regbot\_1mg.slx} around equilibrium with \texttt{linearize()}. Two raw plants drive the design: $G_{wv}(s)$ (motor voltage $\to$ wheel velocity) and $G_{tilt}(s)$ (velocity reference $\to$ tilt angle, with the wheel-speed loop closed). $G_{tilt}$ has one RHP pole at $\approx +8.9\,\mathrm{rad/s}$ -- the falling mode -- so the Nyquist criterion $Z = N + P$ requires one CCW encirclement of $(-1,0)$ for closed-loop stability.
