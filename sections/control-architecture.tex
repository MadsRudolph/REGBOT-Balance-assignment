\section{Control Architecture}
\label{sec:architecture}

\paragraph{Cascade.} Position $\to$ velocity $\to$ tilt $\to$ wheel-velocity $\to$ motor voltage. Each outer loop sees the closed-loop response of everything inside it as its plant. The two raw plants we identify by linearising \texttt{regbot\_1mg.slx} are $G_{wv}(s)$ (motor voltage $\to$ wheel velocity, used for Task~1) and $G_{tilt}(s)$ (velocity reference $\to$ tilt angle with Task~1 closed, used for Task~2). $G_{tilt}$ has one RHP pole at $\approx +8.9\,\mathrm{rad/s}$ -- the falling mode -- so by $Z = N + P$ Nyquist requires one CCW encirclement of $(-1,0)$.

\paragraph{Shared design recipe.} Tasks~1, 3, and~4 follow the same six-step PI-Lead procedure from Lec.~8/9; each task section then plugs in its own numbers.

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{rl}
\textbf{R1.} & Identify plant -- linearise around equilibrium, read off poles, zeros, DC gain. \\
\textbf{R2.} & Pick $\omega_c$, $\gamma_M \geq 60^{\circ}$, $N_i = 3$. \\
\textbf{R3.} & Place the PI zero at $\tau_i = N_i/\omega_c$ (phase contribution $\arctan(N_i) - 90^{\circ} = -18.4^{\circ}$ at $\omega_c$). \\
\textbf{R4.} & Phase balance -- if combined plant$+$PI phase $< -180^{\circ} + \gamma_M$ at $\omega_c$, add a Lead. \\
\textbf{R5.} & $K_P = 1 / |C_{\mathrm{shape}}(j\omega_c)\,G(j\omega_c)|$ -- crosses $0\,\mathrm{dB}$ at $\omega_c$. \\
\textbf{R6.} & Verify with \texttt{margin(L)}, closed-loop step, hardware. \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Task~2 (balance) prepends two extra steps because $G_{tilt}$ has an RHP pole: a sign flip and a ``post-integrator'' at $|G_{tilt}|$'s magnitude peak (Method~2). The standard recipe then runs on the reshaped plant.
